Rule #1: Role Playing

Participating in roleplay is encouraged, as we are a role-playing server. The amount of RP may heavily vary situation-to-situation, but should remain a constant element.

Disregarding RP entirely is generally unacceptable. Wordlessly and emotelessly getting into a fight is an example of disregarding RP.

We aren’t asking you to write paragraphs about how you sneeze or roll a pair of fuzzy dice to see your odds of breathing properly, but attempt to maintain the atmosphere of the server whenever possible.

-

Rule #2: Character Backstory/Naming/Records

Your character's backstory should make sense within Fallout lore.

Character naming should always be taken seriously, as this is an RP server, and you should attempt to follow the below when it comes to naming.

●       NO: Naming a character based off something else (e.g., Peter Griffin, Michelle Obama, Gordon Freeman, etc.) *This rule doesn't apply if you're the creator of said character

●       NO: Naming a character that sounds vaguely similar or is in reference to another character (e.g., Tonald Drump, Griffin Peter, Freedon Gorman, etc.)

●       NO: Naming characters in a way that signifies a lack of RP. (e.g., Chad Chadson, Orange Fluffytail, Killer Jack, etc.) *Note, this doesn't apply to Raiders.

●       NO: Adding titles to your character's name, (e.g., Doctor/Dct., Mr./Mrs., Jr./Sr., ect.) *Using things like "Jack Moore IV" is acceptable as long as the number isn't absurdly high.

Rule #3: Stay in Character

IC in IC. OOC in OOC.

 

IC stands for “In Character”, while OOC stands for “Out of Character”.

Being in character is vital to roleplay and game experience. Do not use emoticons and text talk (e.g., “I c u”, “wtf man”, “how r u c:”).

Do not bring anything that is IC related into OOC chat, such as telling everyone in OOC that you are dead, if there is an issue always ahelp rather than immediately jumping to OOC.

Being out of character should only happen in the appropriate OOC channels on discord and when using LOOC/OOC in game, this also means that you should never bring anything OOC related into IC, such as your dislike of another player so you avoid healing them when they come through your clinic, or giving them a harsher sentence in a trial. Conversely, bringing IC hate into OOC/LOOC rants is frowned upon and likely to have you muted.            

-

Rule #4: Metagaming

Communication taking place to share IC information regarding an ongoing round OOCly is a big no. This includes, but isn’t limited to:

●       DMs/Voice Chat regarding ongoing in round happenings.

●       Posting about the ongoing round OOCly.

Rule #5: Self Antagging

Self antagging is the process of carrying out incredibly hostile actions, or generally antagonist-like behavior without reason against your own faction. Unless you have some sort of massive, absolute proper reason to be doing things such as sabotaging vital areas or dumping clean water, it’s self antagging. Use your brain.

As with other rules, if you’re unsure, please, do ahelp for clarification.
